				<li1_a Type = "Description" Sub-Type = "Overview">Core metadata defines the most essential information for a character, used for indexing and retrieval.</li1_a>
				<li1.01 Name = "Name:">The character‚Äôs full given name, recorded formally.</li1.01>
				<li1.02 Name = "Alias:">Common alias, nickname, or symbolic epithet used in Cast Lists or oral traditions.</li1.02>
				<li1.03 Name = "Role:">The primary function or archetypal place the character fills (Beacon, Healer, Arbiter, Mentor, etc.).</li1.03>
				<li1.04 Name = "Area:">The Locale, Feature, or region most associated with the character‚Äôs life or duties.</li1.04>
				<li1.05 Name = "Dialect:">Variation of speech, idiom, or accent shaping how they are perceived.</li1.05>
				<li1.06 Name = "Drive:">The core motivation or philosophy underpinning their choices.</li1.06>
				<li1.07 Name = "Traits:">Key personality descriptors, comma-separated for clarity (loyal, stubborn, curious).</li1.07>
				<li1.08 Name = "Tags:">Sorting filters for indexing characters by theme, role, or status (e.g., luminous, mentor, retired).</li1.08>
				<li1.09 Name = "Affiliation:">Association with a Major Organization, Steward Family, or other group. *REQ: WhatIs?Affiliation_Type.*</li1.09>
				<li1.10 Name = "Remark:">A short blurb for overviews, cast lists, or indexes.</li1.10>
			</Detail1>

			<li0_a Type = "Description" Sub-Type = "Overview">Character Entry Template Usage Insstructions:</li0_a>
			<li.d Title="Resolve REQs">Answer `"WhatIs?Affiliation_Type"` and `"HasCompanion?"` before beginning. These determine which optional modules are included.</li.d>
			<li.e Title="Populate Core Metadata">Complete the `<Character>` opening tag with key details for indexing and context.</li.e>
			<li.f Title="Expand Core Elements">Fill nested sections such as `<Description>`, `<Background>`, `<Traits>`, `<Strengths>`, etc.</li.f>
			<li.g Title="Affiliation Integration">Populate `<Affiliation>` based on `"WhatIs?Affiliation_Type"`. Characters may have multiple affiliations (e.g., organizational, political, historical).</li.g>
			<li.h Title="Companion Inclusion (Optional)">If `"HasCompanion?" == True`, include a `<Companion>` block using the companion template.</li.h>
			<li.i Title="Quotes & Dialect">Include 1‚Äì3 example quotes. These capture voice, worldview, and linguistic nuance.</li.i>
			<li.j Title="Identity Point">Include demographic context (*Age, Gender, Pronouns*) within the `<Description>` block.</li.j>
			<li.k Title="Optional Branches (Advanced)">Extend entries with additional `<Affiliation>` types (e.g., `"IsDeceased"`, `"HmstdCouncil"`, `"GenAssembly"`) or specialized modules. These can be added later without altering the base template.</li.k>
			<li.l Title="Cross-References (Advanced)">Cross-links to related indexes (Fauna, Flora, Locale, Events) can be generated as separate modular documents, forming part of a multi-index environment.</li.l>
		</Guide>

			<Description>
				The Character Entry Template is the foundational structure for all character documentation within the Narrative Framework. 
				It defines the canonical format and ordering of metadata, descriptive elements, and narrative fields required to portray a character in a consistent and indexable way. 
				All entries‚Äîregardless of affiliation, role, or complexity‚Äîbegin from this template. Nested elements such as `<Description>`, `<Traits>`, `<Background>`, and `<Quotes>` provide a structured space to capture the identity, motivation, relationships, and voice of a character. 
				This section serves as the blueprint from which every Character Entry is built, ensuring interoperability across systems and predictable output for downstream processing.
			</Description>

		<Section2 Title="Character REQ; Affiliation_Type">
			<Description>
				This section defines the core **requirement** for every Character Entry: at least one `<Affiliation>` type. 
				Affiliations act as the primary linkage point between characters and the larger societal, political, or organizational structures of the world. 
				They determine the context in which a character operates, the roles they fulfill, and the relationships that shape their narrative significance. 
				The Affiliation schema provided here establishes the base set of recognized organizations‚Äîsuch as *The Luminary*, *The Pact*, and *Tinkers Union*‚Äîand can be expanded with additional types as the world develops. 
				Because the Affiliation Type is essential for indexing and cross-referencing, it must always be resolved before a Character Entry can be considered complete.
			</Description>
			<Affiliation Type = "TheLuminary">
				**Posting**:  (Current post assignment, including Locale/Feature, purpose, and scope.)

				**Duty**:  (Specific functional role at the Posting‚Äîeducation, mediation, recordkeeping, governance, etc.)
				**Education**:  (How they teach or were taught, styles or philosophies of learning they follow or promote.)
				**Community Engagement**:  (How they interact with or influence the broader community outside their Luminary-specific duties.)
				**Musical Traits**:  (Singing voice, instruments, musical habits, and how those show up in rituals or downtime.)
				**Symbolic Role**:  (What they *represent* in the community‚Äîe.g., light in dark times, stability, firestarter, listener.)

		<Section3 Title="Character REQ: OPTION">
			<Description>
				This section defines optional modules that may extend or enhance a Character Entry beyond the required template. 
				Options allow creators to add depth or specificity without altering the core structure, enabling modular growth over time. 
				The primary optional module currently supported is the `<Companion>` template, which documents bonded creatures or entities and their narrative relationship to the character. 
				Future optional modules may include expanded treatments of relationships, legacies, or specialized roles. 
				These components are additive rather than required, providing flexibility for complex storytelling while maintaining consistency with the base format.
			</Description>
