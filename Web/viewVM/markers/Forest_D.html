Northwood Forest

Overview

  • Divisions:
    • Northern Northwood
    • Southern Northwood
    • Central Northwood
    • Eastern Northwood

Northwood Forest is a vast expanse in the northern part of The Valley, characterized by its temperate boreal climate and diverse terrain ranging from mountains to wetlands.

Divisions and Descriptions

D1: Northern Northwood

Location and Boundaries:

  • Borders the Northern Mountains to the north.
  • Elevation increases as the forest merges into mountainous terrain.

Climate and Soil:

  • Climate: Cooler temperatures due to higher elevation. Harsh winters with heavy snowfall.
  • Soil: Rocky and thin, well-drained but low in nutrients.

Tree Composition:

  • Coniferous Dominance (80%): Pine and Spruce thrive in cooler, mountainous conditions.
  • Birch (15%): Present in sheltered areas.
  • Hardwoods (5%): Oak and Hazel in protected valleys.

Features:

  • Terrain: Rugged with steep slopes and rocky outcrops.
  • Clearings: Rare; primarily natural meadows or avalanche paths.
  • Wildlife: Includes mountain goats, lynx, and alpine birds.

D2: Southern Northwood

Location and Boundaries:

  • Borders Mirror Lake, Willow Stream, and Silver Marshes to the south.
  • Transitional zone between forest and wetlands.
